Who
I Am

Most people my age are figuring out what they want to do. I'm already building it. I've shipped an AI credit engine that a national bank actually uses, designed financial models that adapt to market chaos in real-time, and founded a consulting chapter that went from zero to live client engagements in under three months.

I'm a second-year B.Tech student at IIPE, but the classroom is honestly the smallest part of my education. The real learning happens when I'm neck-deep in a hackathon at 3 AM, stress-testing a model that needs to work by morning, or convincing a room full of judges that my team's solution isn't just clever — it's deployable.

What drives me? The gap between what AI can theoretically do and what it actually does in the real world. I want to close that gap — in finance, in energy, in any system where better intelligence means better outcomes for real people.
